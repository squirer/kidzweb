Vector

The Vector's action was originally developed by French engineer Renaud Kerbrat. This action, the so-called KRISS Super V System (KSVS), is an articulated mechanism which allows the block and bolt to recoil off-axis into a recess behind the weapon's magazine well.[9][not in citation given] The Vector family of weapons is the first to use this action; the company claims the .45 ACP chambering was chosen to demonstrate that the action could "tame" such a powerful round.[10] Variants chambered for the .40 S&W and 9Ã—19mm Parabellum round are currently in development. All KRISS Vectors operate with standard Glock magazines with the .45 ACP caliber weapons using the 10 and 13-round G-21 magazine.
